Have you ever imagined standing in the middle of a vast, golden savanna surrounded by exotic trees and wild deer, with a majestic mountain standing tall in the background? You don't have to fly all the way to Africa to experience this. Welcome to **Baluran National Park**, a wildlife conservation haven affectionately called **"The Africa van Java."**

Located at the border of Banyuwangi and Situbondo, Baluran offers an unforgettable, magical safari adventure. Here are the top highlights you must explore:

## 1. Bekol Savanna & Observation Tower
**Bekol Savanna** is the beating heart of Baluran National Park. This 300-hectare natural grassland changes dramatically with the seasons. In the dry season, it turns into a stunning golden-brown plain resembling Kenya's savannas. In the wet season, it transforms into a lush, vibrant green carpet.

To get the best view, climb up the 3-story **Observation Tower** on the small hill behind the Bekol resort office. From the top, you get a breathtaking 360-degree panorama of the savanna, the blue Bali Strait in the distance, and Mount Baluran standing guard over the area.

## 2. Amazing Wildlife
Baluran is home to hundreds of protected wildlife species. The most iconic is the **Javanese Bull (Banteng)**, the symbol of the park. You will also easily spot:
- **Timor Deer:** Roaming the grass or drinking from waterholes.
- **Green Peacocks:** Displaying their beautiful feathers under the trees.
- **Long-tailed Macaques:** Very active, especially near the beach.
- **Wild Water Buffaloes & Feral Cats:** Occasionally stepping out from the bushes.

*Tip: Visit in the early morning (06:00 - 08:00 AM) or late afternoon (03:30 - 05:00 PM) when the animals are most active and gather in the open savanna to drink.*

## 3. Peaceful Bama Beach
A 3-kilometer drive from Bekol through a rocky forest trail brings you to **Bama Beach**. It features clean white sand and very calm, shallow waters. A wooden path on the west side takes you into the serene **Bama Mangrove Forest**, offering a peaceful walk under the shade of giant mangroves. Be careful with your bags, as the monkeys here are very curious!
